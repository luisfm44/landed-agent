# Closed-back headphones

Closed-back headphones are designed to isolate the listener from external noise and reduce sound leakage.

## Best use cases

- Recording vocals or instruments
- Monitoring
- Podcasting
- DJ monitoring
- Working in noisy environments
- Production in untreated rooms

## Limitations

Closed-back headphones can sound more confined than open-back headphones. They may not provide the same sense of space, imaging, or natural soundstage preferred for critical mixing and mastering.

## Buying advice

Choose closed-back headphones when isolation matters. Choose open-back headphones when soundstage and mixing accuracy are more important and the room is quiet.
